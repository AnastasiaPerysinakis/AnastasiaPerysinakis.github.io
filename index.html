"Designing systems that travel with the patient"

I work on health systems, data portability, and the design of patient-centred infrastructure. My research focuses on how fragmented records and poor interoperability affect continuity of care, particularly in mobile and high-need populations.

My broader interests sit at the intersection of healthcare, machine learning, and systems engineering, with a focus on building tools that are clinically meaningful and operationally usable.

Personal Projects

My work focuses on building systems that reduce administrative friction and improve continuity of care. I am currently developing Global Health Passport, a platform for translating and structuring medical records across borders.

Alongside this, I am strengthening my foundations in machine learning and systems design, with a focus on real-world healthcare applications.
